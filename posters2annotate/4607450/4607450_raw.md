# Biochemical-genetic Analysis of the Effects of Chlorpyrifos and Cypermethrin Insecticides on Zebra Mussels (Dressena polymorpha Pallas, 1771)

Ivan Y. Stoyanov, Teodora A. Staykova, Penka L. Vasileva, Evgeniya N. Ivanova

Section of Genetics, Department of Developmental Biology, Plovdiv University "Paisii Hilendarski", 24 Tzar Assen Street, 4000 Plovdiv, Bulgaria

DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.4607450  
Presented at the 5th Balkan Scientific Conference on Biology

## Introduction

Electrophoretic studies of genetically determined biochemical polymorphism of proteins are used to study the relationship between the level of protein variability and the peculiarities of habitat conditions. Isoenzymes are indicators of gene expression and reveal the characteristic features of gene regulation. The study of isoenzyme variability allows study of genome regulation and contributes to determining the genetic basis of adaptation.

In the present study, a biochemical-genetic analysis was performed to determine the relationship between isoenzyme polymorphism and the zebra mussel Dressena polymorpha body's response to the harmful effects of the pesticides Cypermethrin and Chlorpyrifos.

## Materials

Individuals of the species Dressena polymorpha examined by biochemical-genetic analysis. Exposed to short-term (96 hours) and long-term (30 days) exposure with different concentrations of the broad-spectrum insecticides Cypermethrin and Chlorpyrifos.

Concentrations tested: Maximum Residue Levels (MRLs) according to Directive 2013/39/EU, plus 50% and 30% of the MRLs.

## Electrophoresis Analysis

Samples examined by electrophoresis in polyacrylamide gel by the method of Maurer (1971), with modifications according to Ivanova (1996). Studied: Soluble proteins (SP), malate dehydrogenases (MDH), malate enzymes (ME), and superoxide dismutases (SOD).

## Results

Malate dehydrogenase (EC 1.1.1.37):
Four fractions with different electrophoretic mobility visualized. Genetic control by three loci: Mdh-1, Mdh-2, and Mdh-3. Weaker expression of Mdh-1 locus product observed in 96-hour sample treated with 30% Chlorpyrifos.

Malate enzyme (EC 1.1.1.40):
Multiple molecular forms suggest gene control of two polymorphic loci: Me-1 (alleles Me-1^100 and Me-1^90) and Me-2 (alleles Me-2^100 and Me-2^72).

Superoxide dismutase (EC 1.6.4.3):
Four electrophoretic zones; genetic control by four loci: Sod-1, Sod-2, Sod-3, Sod-4. Two-allele polymorphism at Sod-1, Sod-2, Sod-3.

Soluble proteins (SP):
Six zones (SP-1 to SP-6). Synthesis controlled by at least six loci: Sp-1 to Sp-6. Polymorphism found at Sp-3, Sp-4, Sp-5.

Nine variable loci identified in total.

## Discussion

Probable genetic control of four enzyme and protein groups was determined. Nine variable loci identified to characterize the degree of genetic heterogeneity in zebra mussel populations.

Different intensity of expression of allelic products reported in individuals exposed to short- and long-term pesticide exposure. The observed variability in superoxide dismutases, malate dehydrogenases, and soluble proteins gives reason to accept these systems as markers for reporting the negative impact of tested pesticides.

Allelic products with altered expression are probably related to protection of the body from harmful effects. Increased levels of expression in experimental vs. control samples are associated with anthropogenic stress.
