## Introduction & Objectives

- Squamous cell carcinoma of the head and neck (SCCHN) is a group of cancers derived from the mucosal epithelium in the oral cavity, pharynx, and larynx.
- Treatment options for patients with recurrent/metastatic (R/M) SCCHN in Italy include targeted therapy (cetuximab) or immunotherapy, in combination with chemotherapy.
- The EXTREME trial (NCT00122460) demonstrated improved overall survival (OS) for cetuximab plus platinum and 5-FU (EXTREME regimen) compared with platinum and 5-FU alone.
- The TPExtreme trial (NCT02268695) compared the EXTREME regimen with the TPEx regimen (cetuximab plus platinum and docetaxel), suggesting improved OS for TPEx over EXTREME (not statistically significant).
- KEYNOTE-048 trial (KN-048, NCT02358031) demonstrated improved OS with pembrolizumab + chemotherapy (P+CT) versus EXTREME in the total population, CPS ≥20, and CPS ≥1 subgroups. P+CT did not provide a benefit versus EXTREME in the CPS <1 subgroup.
- A post hoc analysis showed the survival benefit in CPS 1–19 was less pronounced than in CPS ≥20 (median OS: 12.7 vs 14.7 months).
- In Italy, P+CT is the recommended immunotherapy-based regimen for patients with R/M SCCHN with CPS ≥1.
- Study objective: estimate cost-effectiveness (CE) of P+CT versus cetuximab-containing regimens (CCRs) for patients with R/M SCCHN and CPS 1–19 in Italy from an Italian National Health Service perspective.

## Methods

- A three-state partitioned-survival model was developed to estimate the CE of P+CT versus EXTREME and TPEx.
- Health states: progression-free disease (PFS models) and progressed disease (difference between PFS and OS models).
- Comparative efficacy from ITT population of four trials: EXTREME, TPExtreme, GORTEC 2008-03 (NCT01289522), CheckMate-651 (NCT02741570). For P+CT, CPS 1–19 subgroup data from KN-048.
- Survival models selected by statistical goodness-of-fit (AIC/BIC): exponential, generalized gamma, Gompertz, log-logistic, log-normal, Weibull.
- Costs from Italian National Healthcare Service; pharmaceutical list prices from Farmadati Italia; treatment administration from National Tariff Nomenclator; adverse events from DRG fees.
- Outcomes: costs, QALYs, and incremental cost-effectiveness ratio (ICER).
- Probabilistic sensitivity analyses (PSAs) also performed.

## Results

Table 1: Base case cost-effectiveness results (P+CT versus EXTREME)
- EXTREME: Total Costs €86,407 / QALYs 1.634 / LYs 2.508
- P+CT: Total Costs €111,501 / QALYs 1.207 / LYs 1.673
- Incremental: Costs €25,095 / QALYs –0.428 / LYs –0.836 → P+CT Dominated

Table 2: Base case cost-effectiveness results (P+CT versus TPEx)
- TPEx: Total Costs €106,842 / QALYs 1.483 / LYs 2.081
- P+CT: Total Costs €118,834 / QALYs 1.207 / LYs 1.673
- Incremental: Costs €11,993 / QALYs –0.277 / LYs –0.409 → P+CT Dominated

Table 3: Scenario analysis results (P+CT vs EXTREME and TPEx)
- Use EXTREME arm of TPExtreme trial for CCR efficacy: Dominated
- Use EXTREME arm of EXTREME trial for CCR efficacy: €437,830/QALY
- Use EXTREME arm of CheckMate-651 trial for CCR efficacy: Dominated
- Use GORTEC 2008-03 trial for CCR efficacy (TPEx arm): €384,550/QALY
- Use EXTREME arm of KN-048 trial for CCR efficacy: €132,359/QALY

PSA results demonstrated substantial spread in estimates for costs and QALYs, generally suggesting better outcomes and lower costs for CCRs versus P+CT.

## Discussion

- For patients with R/M SCCHN and CPS 1–19, CCRs can represent more effective treatment options, likely cost-effective in Italy vs P+CT.
- Across all scenario analyses, costs were lower for CCRs.
- Limitation: Efficacy results for ITT population of EXTREME, TPExtreme, and GORTEC 2008-03 trials were assumed to describe CPS 1–19, as CPS stratification data were lacking.
- Further research could establish more accurate estimates of relative effectiveness for patients with R/M SCCHN and CPS 1–19.

## Conclusions

- For R/M SCCHN patients with CPS 1–19, analyses demonstrate that CCRs can represent more effective treatment options, which are likely cost-effective in Italy vs P+CT.
- Across all scenarios, CCRs demonstrated cost savings; in some scenarios CCRs yielded more QALYs.
- The robustness of the current analyses indicates the overall CE results would not change.
