## The RECONS Program

M dwarfs comprise three-quarters of all stars (Henry et al. 2006; 2018), and host a plethora of exoplanets presently being revealed. The REsearch Consortium On Nearby Stars (RECONS: www.recons.org) has spent over two decades studying nearby red dwarfs.

This work focuses on:
- Long-term M Dwarf spot activity cycles (SURVEY) — analogous to the 11-year solar cycle
- Magnetic activity behavior of 36 M dwarf "twin" binaries (TWINS)

The RECONS program obtains differential VRI photometry and astrometry observations using the SMARTS 0.9m at the Cerro Tololo Inter-American Observatory. Data acquired 4–6 times per year; photometric precision limit ~7 millimagnitudes (mmag). Coverage spans more than 20 years.

Note: SMARTS time is available on the 0.9m for $600/night.

References: T. J. Henry et al. 2006, AJ, 132, 2360; 2018, AJ, 155, 265.

## A Tale of Two Samples

SURVEY: A volume-complete sample of 463 stars all within 16.7 parsecs and south of 0° declination. N_Survey = 463.

TWINS: 36 common proper motion M dwarf twin wide binaries (72 total stars) compiled from Gaia DR2. Components in each pair have BP, RP, J, H, and Ks all differing by less than 0.1 mag. Estimated masses differ by <3%. N = 72.

## M Dwarf Stellar Cycles – SURVEY

About 20 confident stellar cycles uncovered in fully convective M dwarfs — the largest such collection. Results are pending final analysis and publication.

Four example detected cycles:
- GJ1061 @ R: P = 16.6⁺²⁻¹ yrs, Δ = 45 mmag — Gaussian Process model combining short-term rotational modulation and long-term stellar cycles
- GJ234AB @ R: P = 6.1⁺¹⁻⁰·⁷ yrs, Δ = 25 mmag — Gaussian Process model
- WT460AB @ I: P = 28.9 yrs, Δ = 48.3 mmag — Lomb-Scargle periodogram
- GJ358 @ V: P = 12.5 yrs, Δ = 66.9 mmag — Lomb-Scargle periodogram

## M Dwarf Twin Binaries – TWINS

Twin binary components have similar estimated masses, photometry, age, and metallicity. Examining magnetically relevant tracers in each twin pair to determine if they show similar or different behaviors.

Observations:
- Long-term variability and rotation rates: CTIO/SMARTS 0.9m
- Hα activity: CHIRON at CTIO/SMARTS 1.5m
- Speckle imaging: SOAR 4.1m (check for unresolved companions)
- X-ray: Chandra (scheduled for 4 pairs)

Observed magnetic activity differences in 4 twin binaries:
- Top: Hα line strength comparisons showing significant differences
- Bottom Left: Photometric activity differences for a twin pair sustained over 7 years
- Bottom Right: Chandra X-ray data showing ~factor of 3 difference in X-ray flux for a pair

These "misbehaving" twins will offer excellent testing grounds to examine and constrain dynamo theory.
